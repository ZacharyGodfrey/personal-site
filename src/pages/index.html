A Focus on Understanding

In every position I've held, one of my main focuses has been sharing knowledge and increasing understanding. I believe that the less my work gets called magic the better! I love explaining complex technical concepts in simple terms for non-technical people. I've found that bridging the gap between engineers and non-engineers brings many benefits to my team and anyone who interacts with us.

When it comes to code, it's not enough to solve the problem; the solution must be written in a way that won't feel cryptic to others (or my future self). When the code is easy to read and follow, new team members can get up to speed faster with a more shallow learning curve, new features are easier to add, bugs are less likely to occur, and when bugs do show up they are easier to find and fix.
